THE MANUFACTURING COMPANY CORE ERP FUNCTIONS

Inventory Management Module

  • Tracks stock levels, locations, and movements across multiple warehouses in real-time. Manages SKUs, serial numbers, and batch tracking for full product traceability. Includes cycle counting capabilities, automated reorder points, and low-stock alerts to prevent stockouts. Supports multiple warehouse locations with inter-warehouse transfer functionality.

Purchasing Module

  • Manages vendor relationships, pricing agreements, and supplier performance metrics. Creates and processes purchase requisitions and purchase orders with multi-level approval workflows. Tracks order status from creation through receipt and includes automated three-way matching between purchase orders, receipts, and invoices.

Manufacturing/Production Module

  • Manages bills of materials (BOMs) defining components and raw materials needed for finished goods. Controls production orders, work orders, and job scheduling across production lines. Tracks work-in-process inventory and labor costs, with quality control checkpoints built into production workflows. Includes capacity planning and shop floor control functionality.

Sales Order Processing Module

  • Creates and manages customer sales orders with pricing, discounts, and terms management. Performs real-time inventory allocation and availability-to-promise calculations. Generates pick lists, packing slips, and shipping documentation while tracking order fulfillment status from quote to delivery.

Purchase Order Processing Module

  • Automates PO creation from approved requisitions or reorder points with vendor selection based on pricing and lead times. Manages receiving processes including partial receipts and quality inspection holds. Handles returns to vendors, discrepancies, and integrates with accounts payable for invoice processing.

Reporting and Analytics Module

  • Generates inventory valuation reports, turnover analysis, and aging reports for both stock and orders. Creates purchasing reports including spend analysis, vendor performance, and purchase price variance. Provides production efficiency metrics, manufacturing cost analysis, and yield reports alongside sales performance dashboards showing order trends, customer analysis, and revenue reporting.

THE MANUFACTURING COMPANY JOURNEY IN JDE

1. MASTER DATA SETUP

Before any transactions occur, the company establishes foundational data:

  • F0101 - Address Book Master: Stores all business partners including customers, suppliers, employees, and company locations
  • F4101 - Item Master: Contains all item information including raw materials, components, work-in-process, and finished goods with specifications, costs, and attributes
  • F4102 - Item Branch/Plant: Defines item behavior at specific branches including lead times, order policies, and planning data
  • F41021 - Item Location: Tracks bin locations within warehouses for each item
  • F0006 - Business Unit Master: Stores branch/plant information and organizational structure
  • F0101 - Supplier Records: Vendor master data with payment terms, contact info, and certifications

2. SALES ORDER ENTRY - THE CUSTOMER ORDERS

A customer places an order for 1,000 units of finished product “WIDGET-A”:

  • F4201 - Sales Order Header: Creates the sales order header with customer information, order date, requested delivery date, and shipping instructions
  • F4211 - Sales Order Detail: Records line item details including item number, quantity ordered (1,000), unit price, promised date, and line type
  • F4209 - Sales Order Transaction History: Logs every status change and activity on the sales order
  • F4941 - Sales Detail Work File: Temporary staging for order processing before commitment

During order entry, JDE performs availability check:

  • F41021 - Item Location: Checks on-hand quantities by location
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Verifies current inventory balances

3. MATERIAL REQUIREMENTS PLANNING (MRP)

The system determines that inventory is insufficient and triggers manufacturing:

  • F3411 - MRP/MPS Messages: Generates planned orders suggesting production of 1,000 WIDGET-A units
  • F3412 - MPS/MRP Detail Message: Provides detailed planning recommendations
  • F3460 - DRP/MPS/MRP Exception Messages: Highlights issues like capacity constraints or material shortages

The planner reviews and firms the planned order:

  • F4311 - Purchase Order Header (for purchased items): Would create POs for raw materials
  • F4801 - Work Order Master File: Creates manufacturing work order for WIDGET-A

4. BILL OF MATERIALS - WHAT’S NEEDED

The system references the product structure:

  • F3002 - Bill of Material Master: Defines that WIDGET-A requires 2 units of PART-B, 3 units of PART-C, and 1 unit of ASSEMBLY-D
  • F30026 - Bill of Material Configurator: Handles configurable items if applicable
  • F0005 - User Defined Codes: Stores BOM types (manufactured, phantom, planning)

5. PURCHASING RAW MATERIALS

MRP identifies shortages of PART-B and PART-C, creating purchase requisitions:

  • F43081 - Purchase Requisition Header: Creates requisition for needed materials
  • F43081 - Purchase Requisition Detail: Line items for each material shortage

After approval, requisitions convert to purchase orders:

  • F4301 - Purchase Order Header: Creates PO to supplier with terms, buyer, and delivery information
  • F4311 - Purchase Order Detail: Line items showing quantities, prices, and delivery dates for PART-B and PART-C
  • F43199 - Purchase Order Tag File: Additional PO information and user-defined fields
  • F43121 - Purchase Order Receiver Summary: Tracks receiving status

6. RECEIVING PURCHASED MATERIALS

Raw materials arrive at the warehouse:

  • F4211 - Sales Order Detail: Updated with receipt transactions (using this table for PO receipts as well in some versions)
  • F43121 - PO Receiver File: Records receipt details including quantity received, date, lot numbers, and quality inspection results
  • F4311 - Purchase Order Detail: Updates received quantities and remaining open quantity
  • F0911 - Account Ledger: Creates accounting entries for received inventory value

Materials are put away:

  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Records inventory receipt transaction increasing on-hand balance
  • F41112 - Item Location Detail: Updates specific bin locations where materials are stored
  • F0902 - Account Balances: Updates inventory asset values

7. WORK ORDER PROCESSING - MANUFACTURING BEGINS

The production supervisor releases the work order:

  • F4801 - Work Order Master: Status changes from “planned” to “released”
  • F3105 - Work Order Routing: Defines manufacturing operations sequence (cutting, assembly, finishing, quality check)
  • F3112 - Employee Time and Attendance: Records labor hours by operation and employee

Materials are issued to the shop floor:

  • F3111 - Work Order Parts List: Lists components required (PART-B, PART-C, ASSEMBLY-D)
  • F3121 - Issue Material: Records material issues from inventory to work order
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Reduces raw material inventory and increases work-in-process inventory
  • F0902 - Account Balances: Updates inventory accounts (reduces raw materials, increases WIP)

8. PRODUCTION OPERATIONS

Workers complete manufacturing operations:

  • F31122 - Time Entry Detail: Records labor hours, machine time, and operation completion
  • F31122 - Labor Reporting: Captures direct and indirect labor costs
  • F1302 - Lot Master: Creates lot numbers for traceability if lot control is enabled
  • F3115 - Shop Floor Scheduling: Updates operation status and actual vs. planned times

9. WORK ORDER COMPLETION

Finished goods are completed:

  • F31114 - Work Order Completions: Records completion of 1,000 units of WIDGET-A
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Creates receipt transaction for finished goods, reduces WIP
  • F0902 - Account Balances: Relieves WIP account and increases finished goods inventory
  • F4105 - Item Cost File: Updates standard or actual costs for the manufactured item
  • F3102 - Manufacturing Cost Summary: Accumulates material, labor, and overhead costs
  • F4801 - Work Order Master: Closes work order when all quantities are completed

10. QUALITY INSPECTION

Before shipping, products undergo quality checks:

  • F3711 - Quality Management Results: Records inspection results and conformance data
  • F3701 - Test Master: Defines quality tests and acceptance criteria
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: May move inventory from inspection hold to available status

11. SALES ORDER FULFILLMENT

Now that inventory is available, the sales order can be fulfilled:

  • F4211 - Sales Order Detail: Status updates to “pick released”
  • F4600 - Pick Slips Header: Generates pick list for warehouse
  • F46011 - Pick Slips Detail: Shows specific items, quantities, and locations to pick
  • F4611 - Shipment Header: Creates shipment record when items are packed
  • F4611 - Shipment Detail: Links shipped items to sales orders

Inventory is shipped:

  • F4211 - Sales Order Detail: Updates shipped quantities and status
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Records inventory reduction for shipped goods
  • F0911 - Account Ledger: Creates revenue and cost of goods sold entries
  • F03B11 - Customer Ledger: Posts customer invoice

12. INVOICING AND ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE

  • F42199 - Sales Order History: Archives completed order details
  • F03B11 - Customer Ledger: Shows outstanding customer balance
  • F03B13 - Invoice Transactions: Detailed invoice line items
  • F03B14 - Payment Information: Tracks when customer pays

13. ACCOUNTS PAYABLE - PAYING SUPPLIERS

For the raw materials purchased earlier:

  • F0411 - Accounts Payable Ledger: Records supplier invoices
  • F0413 - A/P Detail: Line item details of supplier invoices
  • F0414 - Payment Header: Creates payment batch when paying suppliers
  • F0418 - Payment Detail: Individual payment transactions

Three-way matching occurs:

  • F4311 - Purchase Order Detail: Original PO quantities and prices
  • F43121 - PO Receiver File: What was actually received
  • F0411 - A/P Ledger: Invoice amounts for matching validation

14. INVENTORY MANAGEMENT - ONGOING

Throughout all processes, continuous inventory management:

  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Every single inventory transaction (receipts, issues, transfers, adjustments)
  • F41112 - Item Ledger Secondary: Additional transaction details
  • F4115 - Inventory by Location: Current on-hand by location
  • F41112 - Item Location Detail: Detailed bin-level inventory
  • F4141 - Cycle Count: Physical inventory counting processes
  • F41051 - Inventory Constants: System-wide inventory parameters

15. COSTING AND ACCOUNTING

Financial integration throughout:

  • F4105 - Item Cost File: Maintains current costs for all items (standard, average, FIFO)
  • F30026 - Cost Component Add-Ons: Detailed cost breakdowns (material, labor, burden, overhead)
  • F0902 - Account Balances: General ledger balances by account
  • F0911 - Account Ledger: Detailed transaction-level accounting entries
  • F0901 - Account Master: Chart of accounts structure

16. REPORTING AND ANALYTICS

Management reviews performance using data from:

  • F4211 - Sales Order Detail: Sales order backlog and fulfillment metrics
  • F42199 - Sales Order History: Historical sales analysis
  • F4111 - Item Ledger: Inventory turnover and movement analysis
  • F3102 - Work Order Summary: Manufacturing variances and efficiency
  • F31122 - Time Entry: Labor utilization and productivity
  • F4311 - Purchase Order Detail: Purchasing spend analysis
  • F0902/F0911 - Financial Tables: P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow reporting

KEY INTEGRATION POINTS

The beauty of JDE is how these tables integrate:

  • Sales drives manufacturing: F4211 → F3411 → F4801
  • Manufacturing pulls materials: F4801 → F3111 → F4111
  • Purchasing replenishes: F3411 → F43081 → F4301 → F4111
  • Everything hits financials: All inventory transactions → F0911 → F0902
  • Customer satisfaction tracked: F4201 → F4211 → F42199

ADDITIONAL SUPPORTING TABLES

  • F0005 - User Defined Codes: System-wide code definitions (order status, item types, etc.)
  • F9210 - One World Versions: User customizations and processing options
  • F9860 - Batch Control: Manages batch processing and posting
  • F98741 - Next Numbers: Controls document numbering sequences
  • F0101 - Address Book: Referenced everywhere for customer/supplier/employee information

This comprehensive flow shows how a single customer order triggers a cascade of events through purchasing, manufacturing, inventory, and financials—all tracked in JDE’s integrated table structure. Each transaction updates multiple tables simultaneously, maintaining data integrity and providing real-time visibility across the entire enterprise.
